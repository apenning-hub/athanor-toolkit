UPFRONT EMBODIED CARBON

The carbon
you spend once

Operational carbon you can chip away at for sixty years. Upfront embodied carbon — the emissions locked into the concrete, steel, timber and aluminium the day the building opens — you spend once, and you can never get back. Pick an archetype, size it, swap a structural choice, and watch the number move. Every emission factor is read straight from the NABERS Embodied Carbon calculator 2026.1; the material quantities are studio rules of thumb.

A first-pass sketch-design tool — a real LCA (eTool, One Click LCA) and a QS replace it in A2/A3. Feeds the good-design + governance domains of the Project Web.

Choose, size, and swap EF = NABERS 2026.1 · QUANTITIES = RULE OF THUMB

The building is split into seven elements. An archetype sets a starting material for each; the frame, floor and façade levers let you swap the three that matter most and see the consequence instantly. Modules A1–A3 (making the materials) plus A5 (construction waste) — the carbon spent before anyone switches on a light.

A1–A3 = product stage (raw supply, transport to factory, manufacture). A5 = construction stage (here, just material waste). Excluded: A4 delivery to site, and the in-use (B) and end-of-life (C) stages — this tool is deliberately upfront-only. Biogenic carbon stored in timber is reported separately in the checks, never netted off the headline.
