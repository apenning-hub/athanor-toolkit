ARCH5004 / ARCH6004 studio tool

What would it take to run Southwark's own data — storage, streaming and all — locally?

We build water towers and substations sized for a suburb. This tool asks the same question of data: instead of one distant hyperscale campus, what if storage and everyday streaming were federated — local infrastructure, sized to the community it serves?

Southwark is the new inner-Adelaide suburb rising on the 8.4-hectare former West End Brewery site, 2km from the CBD — up to 1,300 homes, targeting a 6-Star Green Star Communities rating. It's used here purely as a real, human-scale population to size a facility against — not a real proposal. Netflix already runs a version of this: physical caching boxes ("Open Connect Appliances") embedded inside ISPs worldwide, which is the real-world model behind the streaming numbers below.

How the numbers work

Storage: total data = population × GB/person × redundancy factor; racks = total data (PB) ÷ storage density per rack. Streaming: peak concurrent viewers = population × peak % ; peak throughput = concurrent viewers × bitrate for the selected quality; appliances = peak throughput ÷ 100Gbps per Open Connect-style appliance (20 appliances ≈ 1 rack). Combined: facility energy = (storage IT load + streaming IT load) × PUE. Water = facility energy × WUE. Carbon = facility energy × grid intensity. Floor space = combined rack-equivalents × ~2m² (the NEXTDC A1 Adelaide technical-space-per-rack ratio). This still excludes general compute/AI workloads — see Step 7.
